About Me

Growing up in Kenya, I witnessed firsthand the challenges of a weak healthcare system, where diseases like malaria, tuberculosis, and HIV remain rampant. These experiences, coupled with my clinical work, have fueled my passion for public health and healthcare equity. Over the past decade, I’ve dedicated myself to addressing systemic gaps in healthcare, advocating for underserved communities, and promoting health education.

My journey has shaped my commitment to improving healthcare systems, training healthcare professionals, and advocating for justice in healthcare access. From my early days in Kenya to my current role as a Registered Nurse and public health advocate, I’ve focused on bridging gaps in healthcare delivery, promoting health equity, and empowering communities. My work is driven by a deep belief in the resilience of the human spirit and the power of community-driven solutions.

Mission Statement & Goals

My Mission

Advancing global health equity by building a movement for greater social justice in the world.

My Goals

  • Create a network that provides for the sustainable well-being of the most disenfranchised.
  • Ensure that clinical trials are inclusive of people from different racial and ethnic groups to understand how they are affected by a particular condition.

Why These Goals

Because health equity means that everyone has a fair and just opportunity to be as healthy as possible.

Solution

  • Social justice
  • Removing obstacles to health for disenfranchised, marginalized, and excluded groups
  • Addressing all determinants of health, not only healthcare
